<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When people talk about AI at work, the conversation usually jumps straight to speed. Faster tasks. Faster decisions. Faster output.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">In my <a href="https://cdn-dynmedia-1.microsoft.com/is/content/microsoftcorp/3528300-TiffanyTreacyInterview" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">recent conversation with Keith Kirkpatrick,&nbsp;President&nbsp;and Research Director&nbsp;with&nbsp;Futurum&nbsp;Group</a>,&nbsp;we spent less time talking about how quickly agents can act—and more time talking about who stays in control, how work&nbsp;actually gets&nbsp;done, and why intelligent apps matter more than ever.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The future of work&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;about&nbsp;humans&nbsp;racing to keep up with machines.&nbsp;It’s&nbsp;about humans stepping into&nbsp;higher-value&nbsp;roles—guiding, shaping, and supervising intelligent systems that&nbsp;operate&nbsp;at scale.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="intelligent-apps-change-the-role-of-the-human">Intelligent apps change the role&nbsp;of the human</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">As agents become more capable, the role of the business user changes. People keep working—but they&nbsp;do less manual work.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Humans are increasingly&nbsp;moving away from step-by-step processes to&nbsp;designing the flow, defining the rules, and deciding where judgment matters&nbsp;in their workflows. Intelligent apps become the place where&nbsp;all&nbsp;that happens.&nbsp;Apps are evolving, becoming intelligent, adaptive&nbsp;operating surfaces where people and agents work&nbsp;together.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">That’s&nbsp;an important&nbsp;shift. Agents&nbsp;are not&nbsp;replacing&nbsp;apps. Agents and apps are working together.&nbsp;Agents now&nbsp;show up inside apps, embedded in places where work already happens, with the right context, data, and governance.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="human-in-the-loop-isn-t-a-checkbox-it-s-an-imperative"><strong>Human-in-the-loop isn’t a checkbox, it’s an imperative</strong> </h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">One of the most practical questions Keith raised was about control:&nbsp;<em>How do organizations decide what agents can do on their own—and when humans need to step in?</em>&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The answer is&nbsp;design.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Take something like processing&nbsp;a&nbsp;request for&nbsp;proposal (RFP)&nbsp;or an insurance claim. An organization might decide that transactions under a certain threshold&nbsp;proceed&nbsp;automatically, while&nbsp;higher-risk&nbsp;cases require review. That decision&nbsp;goes beyond technical limits. It&nbsp;reflects business risk, regulatory requirements, and confidence in the process.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The important part is this: those boundaries are intentional, adjustable, and visible. You&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;hardcode them once and walk away. You refine them as conditions change and as confidence grows.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">That’s&nbsp;what&nbsp;human-in-the-loop&nbsp;really means:&nbsp;putting judgment where it matters most.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="automation-works-best-when-agents-specialize"><strong>Automation works best when agents specialize</strong>&nbsp;</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Another theme we discussed was scale. As organizations move beyond single workflows, they quickly discover that one giant “do-everything” agent&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;hold&nbsp;up,&nbsp;and&nbsp;is&nbsp;likely&nbsp;not&nbsp;the&nbsp;optimal&nbsp;path for impact and scale.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">What does scale is multiagent&nbsp;orchestration.&nbsp;Instead of building one monolithic agent, teams break processes into smaller, specialized agents—each responsible for a specific function. One agent&nbsp;validates&nbsp;data.&nbsp;Another&nbsp;checks records. Another recommends an outcome. Humans oversee the system.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This approach has two benefits. First,&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;more resilient. If something changes, you update one part instead of everything. Second, it creates reuse. An agent built for one process can often support others.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">That’s&nbsp;how automation compounds.&nbsp;Apps, agents, and chat each have a role.&nbsp;Automation works best when you match the right tool to the right task. A mobile app with a barcode scanner is faster when speed matters. A background agent is better when no interaction is needed. And chat earns its place when collaboration, clarification, or exploration is involved. Apps, agents, and chat each have a&nbsp;role,&nbsp;the key is&nbsp;to&nbsp;leverage&nbsp;each&nbsp;option&nbsp;where it makes&nbsp;the most&nbsp;sense.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When these&nbsp;apps,&nbsp;chat&nbsp;and agents&nbsp;work together, work feels simpler—not more complex.&nbsp;This shift creates new opportunities for people.&nbsp;One of the most overlooked impacts of agentic automation is inclusion.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When systems can summarize meetings, surface the right information at the right time, and reduce cognitive load, more people can contribute effectively—regardless of working style.&nbsp;For example, meeting transcripts can allow participants to stay fully focused on the discussion, knowing&nbsp;the notes will be available after the meeting.&nbsp;Intelligent&nbsp;assistance&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;just increase productivity. It lowers barriers.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">That matters. Not as a side benefit, but as a core outcome of better system design.&nbsp;You&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;plan your way into this—you learn by&nbsp;doing.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="the-advice-i-keep-giving-customers-is-straightforward-start-deliberately">The advice I keep giving customers is straightforward: Start deliberately</h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">You&nbsp;can’t&nbsp;whiteboard every scenario. You&nbsp;can’t&nbsp;predict every edge case. You learn by deploying,&nbsp;observing, adjusting, and scaling—with governance in place from the beginning.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The organizations that move fastest&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;reckless.&nbsp;They’re&nbsp;deliberate. They build intelligent apps with clear boundaries, visibility, and accountability—and they evolve from there.&nbsp;</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This shift is already underway. The question&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;whether intelligent apps and agents will change how work gets done.&nbsp;It’s&nbsp;whether&nbsp;you’ll&nbsp;design for&nbsp;that change—or react to it later.&nbsp;</p>
